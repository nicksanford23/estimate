# Milestone: the first fully measured building (2026-07-17/18)

What happened, why it matters, in plain language. Status doc, not normative —
process authority is `FULL_PROCESS_LOCKED.md`.

## What exists as of tonight

**600 Baronne St is the first building ever run end-to-end through the locked
process** — the first complete, honest, to-the-inch accounting of a real
project this company has produced.

- **Scale proven, not assumed:** all four floors verified against printed
  dimensions (drawings are ~0.6% plot-stretched — documented, inside
  tolerance). Founder countersign pending.
- **36 room names resolved into 33 physical surfaces:** the open
  kitchen/dining/living is ONE surface with three names; the deck is one; 4
  stairs and 4 shafts set aside as specialty judgment calls.
- **98 edges measured against confirmed wall lines from the PDF's vector
  data:** 57 pass (≤1.5"), 14 need small nudges, 22 need redraws, 5 places
  the machine honestly cannot identify the boundary and defers to a human.
- **Every measurement carries a proof image** (draft edge + chosen reference
  line + the number in inches) — 120 of them.
- **The founder review queue is live** in the workbench, ranked by severity:
  4 glance-and-accept surfaces, 13 fix-plans to approve, 5 deferrals, plus
  the founder pile (open-floor split, deck, stairs, shafts).

## Why this is the milestone

1. **The quality machine works.** This week three reviewers scored the same
   rooms 31-good vs 11-good vs 1-good. The measuring gate ended every
   dispute with numbers — including catching its own mistakes twice (a
   reference line jumped across a chase; a deck edge measured against the
   wrong line — the "11-inch error" was actually 0.67" from the correct
   reference).
2. **The process is real, not paper.** FULL_PROCESS_LOCKED v1.0 (5
   consultation rounds, 33 accepted corrections between Claude and Codex)
   has now executed on a real building without bending.
3. **The path to training data is open.** Surfaces accepted from this queue
   are the first training-eligible geometry in the project's history —
   with per-edge confirmed references and complete evidence chains.

## What it took (the honest ledger)

- A failed first year (unverified labels, wrong architecture) whose lessons
  became the constitution's rules.
- One week of the current push: vision bake-off → edge inspection →
  three-way audit war → measured gate → locked process → this run.
- ~$1 of GPU spend total. The scarce resource was and remains founder
  review time — every design choice (proof images, ranked queue, one-tap
  accepts) exists to maximize truth per founder-minute.
