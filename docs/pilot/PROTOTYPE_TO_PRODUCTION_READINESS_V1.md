# Prototype-to-production readiness plan V1

- Date: 2026-07-17
- Status: execution and customer-readiness proposal
- Process dependency: `FULL_PROCESS_V5_DRAFT.md`, approved for lock in Codex round 5

## 1. Central decision

Do not wait for a proprietary geometry model before presenting the product to another company.

The first customer-facing product can use rented vision AI plus deterministic checks and qualified human review. The proprietary model later reduces cost, latency, and reviewer work. It is not the prerequisite for proving customer value.

The first external version should be described as an **auditable, human-reviewed flooring takeoff system**, not autonomous estimating.

## 2. Development order

```text
lock rules/schema
  -> project and artifact foundation
  -> upstream page/plan/scale/roster stations
  -> five-surface geometry proof
  -> complete Baronne
  -> repeat on diverse projects
  -> Layer-B estimate/export
  -> paid design-partner pilot
  -> private beta
  -> proprietary model promotion
```

Run three limited parallel lanes:

- geometry truth factory and Geometry Lab;
- project foundation/upstream stations;
- Model 1 label cleanup/evaluation.

Do not start real geometry training until training eligibility is satisfied.

## 3. Stage 0: controlling process and implementation foundation

Target window: Days 1–7.

Required:

- promote the approved V5 process to a locked controlling document;
- reconcile label book and schema;
- update agent instructions/skills;
- define canonical statuses and dependencies;
- preserve immutable source documents;
- create project/run/artifact IDs and append-only history;
- build the project dashboard shell;
- finish the five-surface reference/measurement prototype.

Exit gate:

- one source artifact can be traced through every generated artifact;
- a changed source/scale correctly marks dependents stale;
- no conflicting process document governs implementation.

## 4. Stage 1: internal alpha

Target window: Days 8–14.

Required stations:

- S1.5 plan-set map;
- S1.7 scale/transform gate;
- S2 identity roster;
- Geometry Lab with reliable zoom/pan;
- surface proposal overlay;
- numbered edge evidence;

Required benchmark:

- reference choices verified by founder, Claude, and Codex during calibration;

This stage is for internal use only.

## 5. Stage 2: complete-project proof

Target window: Days 15–25.

Required:

- all relevant Baronne pages and levels mapped;
- complete room-identity denominator;
- complete physical-surface denominator or explicit unresolved states;
- all Baronne surfaces run through criticism, measurement, topology, and human decision;
- Layer-B prototype creates traceable quantities and an exceptions list;
- baseline-versus-new comparison completed;
- at least two additional diverse projects processed far enough to expose transfer failures.

Quality requirements:

- zero silently omitted source pages;
- zero known critical geometry errors marked accepted;
- 100% of accepted surfaces carry source/provenance/status;
- 100% of unresolved surfaces appear in the exceptions list;
- no duplicate masks for multiple identities sharing one surface;
- all coordinate-changing edits revalidated;
- a project can regenerate after a source, scale, roster, or geometry correction.

This stage proves the system works as a project workflow, not just an image demo.

## 6. Stage 3: paid historical proof

Target window: approximately Days 20–30, overlapping Stage 2 only after the internal gates work.

Offer a prospective design partner a paid proof on one completed historical project.

Before the presentation, have built:

- company/project intake;
- stable project identification by name/address;
- plan-set/page gallery;
- page routing and revision/phase view;
- verified scale display;
- room/surface roster;
- whole-floor geometry overlay;
- surface and edge review;
- visible measurements and unresolved exceptions;
- quantity report/export;
- audit/version history;
- basic company access separation and backups.

Do not require:

- our proprietary geometry model;
- zero human review;
- perfect automation across every architect;
- every enterprise integration;
- a self-service free trial.

### First presentation structure

1. Discovery: understand current tools, bid volume, estimator hours, errors, and workflow.
2. Show the original plan set and automatic page organization.
3. Show the floor/surface overlay—not only isolated crops.
4. Click a surface and show its edge evidence and measurement proof.
5. Show an unresolved condition and how the system refuses to guess.
6. Show the quantity report and trace it back to the plan.
7. Compare against the customer's completed takeoff.
8. Agree on correction categories and measured time saved.
9. Offer two live parallel projects and a six-month design partnership.

Never claim that close square footage proves correct geometry. Never hide manual work performed internally.

## 7. Stage 4: supervised design-partner pilot

Target window: Days 26–35 for the first launch, then ongoing improvement.

Scope:

- five paying design partners maximum initially;
- customer submits projects through the system;
- our team monitors every project;
- rented AI may generate proposals;
- qualified review remains mandatory;
- customer sees accepted and unresolved results;
- outputs are labeled with status/version.

Pilot operating targets:

| Measure | Initial target |
|---|---:|
| Source pages preserved/tracked | 100% |
| Accepted surfaces with evidence/provenance | 100% |
| Known critical errors silently accepted | 0 |
| Unresolved conditions shown in exceptions | 100% |
| Historical proof turnaround | Under 7 days |
| Live-project turnaround | Under 24 hours where scope is supported |
| Customer correction categories captured | 100% |

Do not promise a fixed “accuracy percentage” without a defined denominator and qualified reference. Track geometry-gate pass rate, critical omissions, correction time, and customer review minutes.

The economic pilot succeeds when customers repeatedly submit projects and the assisted workflow materially reduces estimator time without concealing risk.

## 8. Stage 5: private beta

Target window: Days 36–60.

Required improvements:

- 3–5 architect/project styles demonstrated;
- reliable onboarding;
- customer/company permissions;
- monitoring and alerts;
- backup and restore tests;
- support workflow;
- usage and cost metering;
- revision and stale-artifact behavior tested;
- standardized Layer-B policy configuration;
- customer-readable exports;
- Model 1 evaluated and deployed only as reversible routing;
- declared service limitations.

Beta targets:

- first project configured in less than one business day;
- supported projects complete without engineering database edits;
- median customer review time trending downward;
- variable cost and gross margin measurable per project/customer;
- no training data accepted outside the S8 eligibility chain;
- every production model/configuration can roll back.

## 9. Stage 6: production-oriented system

Target window: Days 60–90+, dependent on project diversity and customer evidence.

Required:

- repeatable customer onboarding;
- published supported/unsupported plan conditions;
- qualified-review operating capacity;
- tenancy, permissions, audit, backup, and incident procedures;
- stable estimate/export workflow;
- billing and usage controls;
- performance and cost monitoring;
- support ownership and escalation;
- security review appropriate to customer documents;
- model registry, canary, promotion, and rollback;
- sealed-project evaluation for major model claims.

Production does not require our proprietary geometry model if rented AI plus review meets cost, quality, and turnaround requirements. It does require operational reliability and honest exception handling.

## 10. Proprietary model readiness

The first real geometry training run begins only after:

- at least 150 eligible physical surfaces;
- multiple projects and architect families;
- locked label book/schema;
- project-disjoint validation;
- complete evidence and eligibility records;
- a declared rented-AI/vector baseline;
- a model-agnostic bakeoff plan.

The first model is exploratory. Promotion comes later, after held-out and canary performance proves it reduces reviewer work without increasing critical mistakes.

Expected order:

1. diagnostic RunPod smoke;
2. Candidate A prompted-segmentation exploratory run;
3. held-out workflow evaluation;
4. alternative segmentation/vector-refinement bakeoff;
5. canary as an S4 proposal source;
6. limited production promotion with rollback;
7. continued independent criticism and human gates.

## 11. Go/no-go checklist for the first external company

### Must be true

- We can ingest and identify the project correctly.
- All source pages remain available.
- We can identify the active plan/phase/level or clearly block it.
- Scale is verified before inch-based claims.
- The room-identity and surface concepts are visibly separate.
- The customer can see the whole-floor result.
- Every accepted surface is traceable to evidence.
- Errors can be corrected without losing history.
- Unresolved conditions are explicit.
- Quantities trace to locked surfaces and policy version.
- Customer data is separated from other companies.
- Backup and recovery exist.
- We can explain exactly where humans participate.

### Must not be claimed

- “100% automated.”
- “Always accurate.”
- “Schedule area proves the geometry.”
- “Our trained model is running” when rented AI/manual review produced the result.
- “Production-ready for every plan style.”

## 12. Decision rule

Present to another company when the complete historical-project proof works and the customer can verify the result. Begin a paid supervised pilot before proprietary training if necessary. Delay broad self-service selling until the workflow, security, support, and unit economics—not merely the model demo—are ready.
